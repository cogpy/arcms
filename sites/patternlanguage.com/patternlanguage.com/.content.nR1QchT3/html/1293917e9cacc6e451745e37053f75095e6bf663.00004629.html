This is a process for making ornament in any place or thing. It allows you to design and create ornaments by thinking, looking, shaping strong centers and shaping positive space.

The ornament-making process has many steps which go roughly along the following lines:
  1. Look carefully at the building or building component which is to receive the ornament, and find the "gaps" in it.
  2. Ask yourself what overall gesture in the whole seems to repair the empty gap-like feeling in the whole?
  3. Find a simple repeating pattern which is consistent with this gesture and fits within it.
  4. Now improve the ornament, by making the individual bits of space in the ornament more positive. To do this, start giving better shape to individual repeating elements. "Better" means that the negative space between these elements, not only the elements themselves, become well-shaped and positive in shape.
  5. Make appropriate boundaries, which help to unify the ornament, and its relation to its surroundings.
  6. Once you have this clear, try adding a second level of scale, of other smaller shapes, which make the ornament whole, and which increase the unity of its interior space-- what this means is that the space between element become more positive because of these small elements you have added.
  7. Do what you may need to, to add a third level of detail, thus embellishing the centers you have just created with small centers. They should be chosen to create both large centers and small ones. (check tutorial to understand wehat this means).
  8. Once again sharpen these smallest ones, and make them more positive.
  9. If it is helpful, increase contrast, to make the ornament more vivid.
  10. In any case make sure it is not too extreme: it should end up subdued and harmonious.
