Potential Obstacles

To create the structure of living space which makes a city, it is necessary that certain freedoms exist to make the right kind of space, with the right attributes, and the right connectivity, in the right places.

However, several dominant features of present-day urban life, create opposing pressures, which interfere with the process of creating the living structure. These include the following (shown in red).

Here, for illustration, are some possible solutions to these problems (shown blue). Have a look at these problems and needed solutions, and then, DOWN BELOW, vote your choices as to which of these needed solutions you would like to see implemented in Duncanville:

  1. The dominance of transportation engineers and the relative importance given to the car
    • reduce the power and presence of the car
    • reduce the total square footage allowed to be consumed by asphalt surfaces, both on public land and on private land.
    • reduce parking standards
  2. Sometimes negative impact of the operation of developers working under the pure profit motive
    • require that development projects not only contribute to our tax base, but also contribute to the amenity of public space in the city
    • ask for specific conteibution to the formation of valuable public buildings ewhen appropriate.
    • ask for specific contribution of the developer to the formation of coherent pedestrian space in the city, near their projects
  3. Excessive power of the fire department, and the undue dominance of fire-engine related issues
    • reduce power of fire department to increase road widths
    • driveway widths
    • turning radius
  4. Zoning, setback rules, especially setback issues which create a useless ring of unusable space around each house
    • reduce power of fire department to increase road widths
    • driveway widths
    • turning radius
  5. Zoning land use rules about land use which make it more difficult for people to combine community, houses, workplaces in an integrated fashion.
    • reduce power of fire department to increase road widths
    • driveway widths
    • turning radius
  6. The profit motive as it touches choice of land, and people's connection with a place
    • reduce power of fire department to increase road widths
    • driveway widths
    • turning radius
In consideration of these matters, and with the necessity of making changes so that these effects are reduced, thus allowing important things to be done, we ask that you indicate which of these matters -- in your own mind -- are most in need of change, and in which of these constraints and obstacles the city should look for greater change and flexibility.
