KEY ISSUES

The urban fringe is a huge area of major importance in modern cities. It provides the area where nature is most accessible. Yet it is often not natural, partly derelict, not park, yet not agricultural -- too often a zone of devastation.

A process for continuous, piecemeal, management of these areas, to create man-made wild and natural land, has been shown by McClung to work effectively.

  • Keep topography as it is, and keep or enhance spots that are beautiful NOW
  • Leave major trees as centers and clear around them
  • Respect habitat of wildlife, bushes, swamps, rocks, tall grasses
  • Use boundaries and enhance them, as habitat and as ways of strengthening the meadows
