TIMELINES FOR SALEM FIRST 100 HOUSES
DRAFT Houses and house group timelines are accurately depicted. Permissions and infrastructure timelines lack some detail.
BASELINE DATA/DEFINITIONS          
100 houses  size range 1200-3000 sf
Average size of house 2100 sf (my guess, the average may get a bit smaller)
Average cost say $150/sf (construction cost, not including fees, not including cluster infrastructure)
Total Cost:  $32 million house construction, plus professional fees plus local roads sewage etc
In the following "House cluster" refers to a group of 100 houses
"Housegroup" refers to a group of 10 houses within a housecluster
Actual numbers of houses in a group may vary from 5-20
"Generic" for plans or processes refers to those which can be used
many times as for a house, construction details, or housegroup plans
The idea of "generic" includes the idea of endless variation, biological in character
TIMELINE FOR AGREEMENTS ABOUT GENERIC DESIGN AND PATTERNS  
Sep-02 Started website for FSA, CJR, and for families
Oct-02 Diagnosis of 275 acres
To find land that must be protected and best building sites for clusters
Start work on generic patterns for house groups
Start work on generic patterns for houses
Start work on generic house construction system
Work to find suitable "japanese layout method"
Apr-03 Approval of generic patterns and processes by CJR
Apr-03 Approval, in principle, of generic patterns and processes by FSA
TIMELINE FOR MASTER PLAN ACTIONS & CLUSTER DESIGN    
Dec-02 Broad zoning approved
Feb-03 Establish rough position of first cluster (100 houses)
May-03 Broad plan made for first one hundred houses
Involvement by a few families standing in for the whole (as yet unformed) group
Demolition of buildings
Demolition of unwanted infrastructure
may involve expense, time,etc
Mar-03 First ten families available
assigned to first house group
Register families, receive $5000 payments, start monthly ground rent
TIMELINE FOR HOUSEGROUP DESIGN        
Apr-03 First meeting of ten families
Fix position of first housegroup
Lay out first house group with families, on the ground
Place stakes in the ground
Make survey drawing from stakes
Planning permission drawings made
Submit surveyed plat and housegroup plan to Recorder's office
Jun-03 Submit plan of proposed housegroup for Salem planning approval
Establish path for construction vehicles to this housegroup
Very important since this must be chosen to do least harm to land and ecosystem
Put up construction fencing to protect open lands, and prevent construction access across them.
Establish which fields and open land are "related" to housegroup
This may include fields, agriculture, nature, water, trees, etc
Settle husbandry of these fields
Rough first idea plan of planting, water, irrigation, food production, etc
Aug-03 Receive planning approval for housegroup
TIMELINE FOR SINGLE HOUSE DESIGN        
Jun-03 Start of first house design  (staggered start, 1 week apart for each)
The family uses the webtool to get preliminary volume, outdoors and schematic indoors
Detailed design work starts with architect, onsite with each family
Families wishing to design and/or build their own houses are given GIA (go it alone) status
Each family works with CES to get 1/8 inch designs in hardline
Architect prepares drawings to reflect this layout
Variation of materials, construction features, are developed by family and architect together
Aug-03 First house design is finished
Aug-03 Construction drawings submitted to Salem building dept.
Each set consists of unique plan, with generic details
GIA do their own permit work with their own contractor and /or architect
 ("japanese layout method" permits rapid completion)
Oct-03 Designs for all ten houses in the house-group are finished (staggered finish of 1 week apart each)
Oct-03 Building permit issued for first house in group
Dec-03 Get permits for all 10 houses combination of specific configuration drawings and generic details
TIMELINE FOR CONSTRUCTION OF A SINGLE HOUSE    
Nov-03 Start of first house construction
Put in infrastructure for house-group 1
Contractor uses standard working drawings and rules, for all houses except GIA houses
GIA families (Go-it-alone families), take responsibility for their own architect, and their own builder.
 From this moment forward, the GIA families now operate on their own,
except that they are subject to certain conditions of design.
GIA Families who choose to have their own architect or builder go on separate schedule
(must be aware that a too-large number of GIA families would have adverse effect on
schedule and quality of project)
Nov-03 General contractor for the project undertakes all remaining houses on the same cycle, namely:
3 weeks Set and pour foundations
2 weeks Rough-in services
2 months Masonry construction
1 month Framing construction
3 weeks Finish framing
3 weeks Roof and sheathing and finished surface
3 weeks Windows, doors
3 weeks rough in plumbing, electrical; install heating, insulation
4 weeks Sheetrock and/or plaster
3 weeks Finish plumbing electrical
2 weeks Trim
3 weeks Cabinetwork and built-ins
2 weeks Finished floors
3 weeks Paint, cleanup
Oct-04 First house completed
Feb-05 All ten houses in housegroup completed; staggered two weeks apart
TIMELINE FOR CONSTRUCTION OF A HOUSE-GROUP, & ENTIRE CLUSTER  
Nov-03 First house group started
One house group takes about 15 months
House-groups overlap with staggered starts 1 month apart
Assumption: One contractor builds each house group, has two crews for each trade,
Need minimum three contractors of this size for entire cluster
Or a single larger contractor with about 6 complete crews available for each trade
Feb-05 Completion of first house group
Nov-05 Completion of all ten house groups, House Cluster is complete
