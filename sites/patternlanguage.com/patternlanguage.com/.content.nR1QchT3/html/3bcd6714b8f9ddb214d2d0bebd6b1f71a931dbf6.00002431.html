A POEM
MADE BY PEOPLE IN THE DUNCANVILLE COMMUNITY
TALKING TOGETHER AT A WORKSHOP January 21 2003

 

Our comments on the new process of creating the city together.

 

 

David Jones   Respect uniqueness and differences. A collective “WE would like to see…”

 

Steve Madison   Build life from life. I recognized that my girlfriend was the One and started to nurture her and she blossomed.That's what I feel about Duncanville: Nurture it and it will blossom. Duncanville is the one that I desire.

 

Debbie Long   It takes an attitude change. We can work with small steps. Invite everyone to the party

 

Marilyn Massey   Economic development is good if it doesn’t destroy. This is a vision of “Home town” Hometown where we live.

 

Jim MacDonald   Walk the street. Walk your dog at night. Create the environment from those activities.

 

Travis Towns   It doesn’t take a lot of money.

 

Jim O’Gara   We need to spend money to make money. Linkage of physical public space leads to linking social interactions leads to linking economic transactions

 

Song   Do it piece by piece with a holistic end view

 

Tammy Kuykendall   At the end we can say, “we did it”.

 

Ken Weaver   Preserving what we have! The opposite of urban renewal.

 

Jan Davis   Hold on to things which have life.

 

Jim Hewlett   Space is formed by buildings. . . .
Space is formed by buildings.

 

David Green   Thinking outside the Box. Make paths. Make pedestrian links.

 

Glenn Repp   It is good to talk together.

 

Kent Cagle   The workshop validated our intuition. We are on the right track: These are a lot of things people have been talking about..

 

Julia Jobe    Look at the little things
. Now I understand why I feel as I feel when I’m out there. Do action projects not a big Plan. Little things led to big changes .Quit comparing with other cities: Build on what we’ve got. Recognize the goodness of Duncanville. Call the neighborhoods to have similar workshops.

 

Earle Jones     Perceptions have been changed.
