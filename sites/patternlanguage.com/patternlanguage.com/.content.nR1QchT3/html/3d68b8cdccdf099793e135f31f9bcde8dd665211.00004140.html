INDIVIDUAL LETTERS FROM EUROPE AND THE WORLD

From: Katalin Bende, Estergom, Hungary
To: Chris

Subject: Nature of Order

Dear Chris,
I've been thinking of you lately and sometimes wondering how that Book comes along.
 
From time to time I am invited to give lectures at the Budapest University of Technology for future architects and urbanists as well. The latest one was an interesting experience. Architects always want to know about your buildings, urbanists are very interested in your theories. So I thought, it's no joke, I started to read The Nature of Order again and tried my best to interpret the ideas brought forward in it about Order and the man-made world. The students were open, had lots of questions - and I started to have a strong feeling of "Dejavu": you probably have this section in the book somewhere, if not, I certainly heard it from you:
"I think  these are great ideas, but I think it's impossible to implement them now. Isn't the world about something else right now? How about progress?"... etc.
I could feel they were disturbed emotionally, all what they heard made sense to them, yet Could Not accept it. It seemed too close and personal, yet lightyears away from what they hear at the University.
 
So, how far is it? I feel it is more than time to get it out. I hope you were able to make reasonable arrangements with Oxford, and restore peace with Joyce. Hopefully the book is at the printer by now.
 
When I was preparing for the latest lecture I had a heartfelt experience: I was reading the Prologue, where you write about the film (Places for the Soul) and the people in the movie theater; by the end of the chapter I started to cry. I hope the Book will have the same effect on people who does not quite have the same background information.
 
All my best to the work that is left,
and thank you again for shaping my world into what it is now.
Love,
Katalin
