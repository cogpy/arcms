EXPLANATION OF OUR OVERALL APPROACH
AN EVOLVING VIEW OF HOW -- IN FUTURE -- WE MAY CONSIDER CONTINUOUSLY BUILDING AND REBUILDING OUR WHOLE LIVING WORLD
Uniqueness of each place -- the key to everything

The Earth has become damaged. Cities are ugly. 20th-century urbanization has destroyed the countriside. Somehow we must rebuild the earth, repair the damage, and through continuous creation, repair, and reconstruction, create, for ourselves, a living world.

turquoise background designates an active link

How may we rebuild the world? Through small acts, of course, undertaken by thousands -- millions -- of people, slowly, over time, repairing the delicate fabric of the wilderness, of agriculture, of villages, and urbanization. But all these acts small enough to be affected by individuals, and small groups, so that it is manageable for us, together, to undertake this huge task of rebuilding.

How to repair a riverbank, how to make a small garden take on the natural quality of wilderness, how to maintain human access to a mountain wilderness without doing harm, how fish and birds and wild animals may be protected and supported; how, even, to take responsibility for building and rebuilding, small pieces of ecology and of nature itself.

Equally, in the realm of buildings, roads, cars, in the realm of furniture and artefacts, a similar attitude which makes the healing of such things comprehensible, and puts the healing of such things within our reach, so that we understand how to repair them and make them whole.

Even ornament should be a source of pleasure, and a normal completion of many structures at different scales. It provides the life of a building -- even of an individual room or piece of furniture -- by putting in those centers which are called for by context, and then create a natural sensation of completion in the whole.

The interiors of rooms, when made living and comfortable, nearly always have windows beautifully shaped and placed, each window design having its own fitting division into panes; the room may have fireplaces which form centers in important rooms; sources of light that create comfortable and sparkling light. Built-in furniture, where it is provided, is comforting and tailored to the room.

Work may be organized to support a creative life of work, productivity and efficiency. This needs new ways of thinking about the work itself . The interiors of offices and workplaces, too, can be made living and comfortable, when people lay them out for themselves according to their needs, and habits, and sensibilities.

Scattered among these areas are neighborhoods with individual family houses, and nowadays there are more and more small houses for a one or two persons. Houses are placed on the land, with special attention to the way each building volume fits the land. To connect a house well to the neighborhood, each house has an entrance which works as a transition

In the denser parts of a city, there will be many apartment buildings and apartment complexes. At the highest densities the high apartment buildings are typically four and five stories high, or higher; at lower densities the apartment buildings are two and three stories high, and there are also terrace houses and rows of connected terrace houses.

Neighborhood greens, pedestrian networks, and local pedestrian streets and squares provide for comfortable human existence, among well-placed parking (a profound and subtle matter), which provides ways to reach them. Gates and gateways can be large (as in a street) or small (as in a garden wall). Like boundaries, gates play a fundamental role in helping to make the environment coherent.

The boundary zones of cities, where nature and city meet, play a vital role in giving people continuous access to nature, and in helping to integrate the city and the land. These boundary zones can be made of continuously varying meadows, cared-for and managed areas of near wilderness in a natural state.

The core downtown areas of cities, remain fundamental in providing business, entertainment and refreshing human interaction.

All over the world, roads play a huge role both in providing essential access to new economic possibilities, and sadly, too, in destroying the world. Sensitive handling of major regional roads and freeways, bridges, railroads, and other corridors, is therefore vital.

In other parts of the world like India, where densities are high and incomes low, dense but humane communities of small houses, may be conceived and built by families for themselves, creating humane conditions in spite of high density and low cost.

Cities have different densities, of course, and the environment varies greatly according to the densities which work. In urban areas where prevailing densities are mainly two stories, there is one kind of thing. In urban areas where prevailing densities are upwards of four stories there is another kind of thing.

In more rural areas where densities are 1 or 2 stories, and where buildings are scattered, there are more village-like conditions.

Nature -- including cities , neighborhoods , water, parks, agriculture, villages, and wilderness -- covers the earth.

Every kind of creation of living structure, has essentially the same format. The slow formation of St. Marks Square in Venice had this format. Even a painting or drawing of a person's face can be made, by application of a sequence which once again rely on the creation of living centers and positive spaces in the right order.
