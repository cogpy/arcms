Definition of a center

The evolution of a design in your mind's eye, through centers


The small-house design process has five main phases

  • Session 1: Set garden position, house position, and living room
  • Session 2: Define house volume and the inside of the living room
    • Session 3: Place walls, entrance, roof: the substance of the building
    • Session 4: Establish building structure
    • Session 5: Fix outdoor spaces, doors, windows and ornament

Each of these phases is long enough for a single session. You should complete a session, then have a rest, before you start another session.

Session 4: Establish building structure
Steps Action Notes
Step 4a
What are the minor rooms attached to the main room? Can you make a list of them?

Make a list of minor centers that will be placed around the major centers of the house.
Step 4b
Is there a two story part of the house? If so, you must ask yourself where you want the stair, and what shaped volume it will occupy.

Make thre stair a positive three dimensional volume which is simple, rectangular, and beautiful to experience.
Step 4c
Can you now make a simple syncopated grid to serve as the structural grid of the house?

Make structural bays each of which is a center, in its own right. They do not all need to be equal in dimension, but they should be parallel. The asymmetrical grid will be the pattern of the house.
Step 4d
If any room is to have visible beams, try to imagine, as if you were standing in the room, a beautiful pattern of beams which wil make a nice ceiling.

Make each ceiling a positive center with a beautiful design.
