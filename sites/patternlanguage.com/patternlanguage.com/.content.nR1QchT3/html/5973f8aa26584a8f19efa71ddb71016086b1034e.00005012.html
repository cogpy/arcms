POINTS SYSTEM FOR CONTRIBUTION TO THE LIFE OF FAIRVIEW

  1. The idea of the contribution method
    • We ask that every owner, architect, industry, or developer does as much as possible to make the environment of Fairview a better place.
    • This depends on our being able, all over Fairview, the heart of our city, to slowly grow a connected swath of coherent natural-and-built space where people can enjoy themselves
    • We ask that each individual or company who builds, and profits from our community, also makes as big a contribution as possible, to enhance the life of the community, in terms which we have agreed on, and will -- to the best of our ability -- define.

  2. Contributions of quality in the environment
    • We are not asking for money, but for urban quality, as defined below.
    • Our main aim is to create coherent, useful, enjoyable, urban space along the corridor. To achieve this we ask that each developer pay attention to the following issues:
      • Help the evolution of the Main Street environment
      • Help to respond to the diagnosis which the city has adopted (it gets updated every year).
      • Help to incorporate as many patterns as possible on your land.
      • Help reach out to the land (public and private) which is adjacent to your land.

  3. Here are the twelve most important patterns which will help developers contribute to the life of Main Street:
    • Percent of the area dedicated to pedestrian space
    • Positive quality of pedestrian space, achieved by partial enclosure of pedestrian space
    • Using the buildings and building frontage as enclosures to help shape the newly formed pedestrian space
    • Using trees to shape and enclose the newly formed pedestrian space
    • Keep parking invisible, shielded, yet with excellent access where it is needed for commerce
    • Make each building have a beautiful center, which is a target for people to walk towards
    • Street furniture: benches, fountains, sheltered places to sit
    • Places which children can enjoy: not "playgrounds" but something closer to the heart.
    • Looped roads which allow vehicles and pedestrians to mingle.
    • Connectivity, making each pedestrian precinct connect to those nearby, so one can take a pleasant walk for blocks and blocks
    • Use water, and build lakes and streams, creating places where people can walk and sit an enjoy.
    • Broad comfortable sidewalks as places where food stands, musicians, artists, small merchants, vendors, can entertain and provide little extras.

  4. Developer's obligation to early work with the city to achieve these goals in the first conceptual sketches
    • City will provide a sit-down session to show how the point system works
    • Architectural advice to help work out the best way to make a high point score and also attain the developer's objectives
    • Space on the website, for the developer a chance to show Duncanville citizens how he proposes to help the public space of the city along Main Street
    • A chance to build up a positive atmosphere from day one when the developer first has an idea and wants help developing the idea

  5. The healing process: Every developer is required by the city to maximize his points, and in doing so contributes to the life of the city
    • Patterns
    • Points
    • Discussion
    • Help with the design
    • Visible acknowledgment, in public form, a statue or memorial, of the help which each developer has given the public life of the city.
