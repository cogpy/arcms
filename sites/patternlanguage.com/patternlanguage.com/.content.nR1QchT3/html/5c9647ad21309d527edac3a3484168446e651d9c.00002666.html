REALITY EXPERIMENTS
FOR THE PEDESTRIAN NETWORK

    The repeating cycle of the network creation process has six main phases

  1. Diagnosis

  2. Good Connections

  3. What is Available

  4. Choose the one which helps the network most as a whole

  5. Make the space positive

  6. Intensify the new bit of path with centers

1.  DIAGNOSIS

PERFORM THIS EXPERIMENT
The Setting. Walk about your neighborhood. What are the best places for pedestrian paths? What are the worst places?
Find the Existing Centers. Ask yourself, above all the question: Which spots function NOW, as centers, places where you want to be, places in which you feel a sense of beauty, or a sense of conection to the neighborhood as a whole.
Draw on a Map of the Neighborhood. Mark the best centers on a map
Confer with neighbors Go as far as you can in reaching agreement about the best centers
Make communal map Draw the agreements about the best and most important centers on a communal map, which is shared by being available in a public place, or shared by being accessible on a communal website.
