Conclusions of the 2020 Report

In 1994 the largest survey ever made in Stafford county asked people 160 questions about their wishes.

Every single household in Stafford was polled. An unpredented 21% of all households responded, and the 5600 responses represented nearly 18,000 people. The opinions on subjects listed below were nearly unanimous:
Among the more important findings were:

89% said that they wanted to control development.(the context made it clear that they wished to reduce it, control it, and prevent it from damaging the beauty of the county.
84% said that they wanted legal protection to prevent development from damaging historic sites and buildings.
75% said that they believe Stafford is growing too fast now.
75% said that they wanted to keep new development focused on and nearby to existing communities, and that landowners should not be free to build whatever and wherever they like.

95% said that what they want to preserve about Stafford county is its natural beauty, rural character, quiet, and peacefulness.
70% said that preserving the environment is one of their most important issues.

Although these sentiments were made clear in 1994, to this day, seven years later, little or nothing has actually been done to control development. The citizens of Stafford County therefore call upon the Stafford County Board of Supervisors to take action, to begin such a process, without any further delay.

* Figures are taken from the Report on Survey of Stafford Residents' Views, November 1, 1994, 2020 Committee, County Administration Building, Stafford, Virginia 22554
