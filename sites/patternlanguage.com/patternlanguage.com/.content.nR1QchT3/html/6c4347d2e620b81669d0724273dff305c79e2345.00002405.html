PATTERNS KNOWN TO WORK
A FEW OF THE DESIGN AND CONSTRUCTION PATTERNS KNOWN TO HELP BUSINESS AND COMMUNITY CENTERS

  • RAISED WALK

    A pedestrian path along a road with fast-moving cars should be about a foot and a half above with road with a low wall along marking the edge. Keep the cars below a peedestrian's line of sight.

  • PUBLIC OUTDOOR ROOMS

    Make outdoor rooms--partly enclosed places, with some roof, columns, without walls, perhaps with a trellis, Place the outdoor rooms beside important paths and within view of homes, stores, and workshops.

  • 9% PARKING

    In any given area more than 9 percent given to parking iwll ruin the space. Make multiple small parking spaces and often it is best to place them behind a building rather than between the builidng and the street.

  • NIGHT LIFE

    Knit together shops,amusements, and services which are open at night, along with hotels, bars and all-night diners to form centers of night life: well-lit, safe, and lively places that increase the intensity of pedestrian activity at night by drawing all the people to the same few spots in town.

  • SHADE

    Ample places where people can be comfortable, outdoors, in the summer heat. Trees with big canopies, arcades, porches, an the shade of buildings.

  • 30 PERCENT OF ANY AREA DEDICATED TO PEDESTRIAN SPACE

    Ample places where people can be comfortable, outdoors, in the summer heat. Trees with big canopies, arcades, porches, an the shade of buildings.

  • POSITIVE SPACE

    Positive quality of pedestrian space achieved by partial enclosure of the space and good shape of each portion.

  • USING TREES TO SHAPE AND ENCLOSE THE NEWLY FORMED PEDESTRIAN SPACE

    Positive quality of pedestrian space achieved by partial enclosure of the space and good shape of each portion.

  • SHIELDED PARKING

    Keep parking invisible, shielded, yet with excellent access where it is needed for commerce.
