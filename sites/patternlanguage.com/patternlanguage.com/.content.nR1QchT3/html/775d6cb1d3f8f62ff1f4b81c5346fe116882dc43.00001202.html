I went to Marble Colorado this weekend. The white mable quarried there is of quality the equal to that of Carrera, Italy. The Lincoln memorial and the Washington monument were quarried here as were many architectural facades of the early twentieth century. They are currently looking for the perfect block to replace the tomb of the unknown soldier in Arlington. The Quarry is in limited operation today resuming operation in 1991 after being shut down for fifty years. A canadian company recently took over and plans to expand the scale of operations. Locals in the nearby town are nervous.
The quarry is inside the mountain, close to the surface, at about 9,000 feet elevation. The operations have created a huge cavern where room sized blocks are cut from the face of the deposit.
Microscopic sea life with calcium cabonate skeletons make limestone. Under great heat and pressure, in nature, limestone makes marble. Under less heat and pressure limestone is made into portland cement, by people.

Hi Guys,
The Mandelbrot topic is a great lead into the both the subtlties and basic foundations of order, and how the work is accomplished, I think. There's a lot suggested by his discoveries that Gliek has only just opened the door to.

Looking at Order in terms of sequence, and sequences, the same way we do a building project, or a piece of art work, can be a fresh way of seeing it.

In this view, we see Order being built up in stages from the simpler to the more complex, the way we might expect intuitively; the way we seem to see it in our science.
So far so good, and when we get specific about it, things work nicely together and seem to clarify themselves a bit as well.

For example, the force of gravity, and all its effects, can be seen as a problem which was solved early on, in an earlier time. It is part of our order, impossible not to reckon with and take into account, but we are no longer dealing with it in a formative sense, that chapter is behind us. It was accomplished at an earlier time in the sequence.
If we accept a concept like this, that order itself could evolve sequentially, work like Mandelbrot's or our own, takes on a different, potentially more profound meaning.
The implication is difficult to escape that we ourselves, and our work, in its complexity and uniqueness, is the present cutting edge of innovation in the Universe.
We, and our creations, are the
contemporary "live" sequences, if you will.
Gravity, fission, fusion, crystalization, ion exchange, photosynthesis, respiration, enzymatic recognition, membrane diffusion, and all the other wonders of our present platform become "Old News", only tools and materials for further extension and elaboraration of the curious "Life" that lies behind the dynamic, evolving order.

With the begining of this realization a curious thing happens, pychologically. Basically the taboo against the spiritual or religious consideration kicks in...
"The suggestion of something behind it all has already been covered by religion, and its a mess we don't want to get into , besides, since faith has no datum, the question will always remain unanswerable. Science has succeeded precisly because it has stuck to the data and kept itself value-free, let's not threaten that."

That's essentially what we're up against.
The amazing irony is that it would be rediculous to try and make a religion out of this view of order, because, simply stated, it already IS one.
Everything, not nothing, is its datum. It all points in the same direction, it contains unmistakable aesthetics and ethics, and it is unfinished. And we are at the center of it as the undisputed most powerful high intensity points of order in the observable Universe.
If that's not enough to evolve a spiritual responsibility toward each other and the planet, then, sorry:
"It ain't us, babe!"

Buenas Tardes Compadres,

As we continue to look more deeply into the nature of order and how it accomplishes the work, we are slowly boxed into a position of acknowledging a Trajectory to the observable present, and the reconstructed history. In fact, this is the most noble goal of our exploration.

This evolution is inescapable for us as interprative thinkers.

Many positions have and will continue be taken regarding this imperative, however the bar of the standard of acceptance continues to rise as we find out more and more about how the Universe is put together.

This overarching context is important to support occasionally even though it poses the most profound questions, which we are not yet graceful at dialouging to without resorting to belief or skepticism. Keep in mind that the Human reality is that of an emerging primitive thinker, and in need of compassionate understanding, from a Universalist's point of view.

If your work progresses a short way into the study of Order, you must open the study up as you go. There is no part of the observable or imaginable which is exempt, it all bears on every aspect of itself.

In the end it becomes the study of interconnections on all levels, between all things, at all times. Order and it's subsets, functions, and items, are all related, and interrelated. No meaningful ideas can exist without quickly referencing those relationships.

This is a key, seldom acknowledged, reality and concept, that defines Order at all times and places.

It describes the starting and ending point of any serious inquiry.

We skip lightly into this study initially, fascinated by the breadth and depth.
Only later do we feel the responsibility that some of the larger Truths load onto our view of life and the human endeavor.

Like the little student piped up when told it was the "study of Totality";..."You mean everything?".

i'll cover many points at once, writing in a separate
program since the input window drives me mad...

- Rob invited me to the DreamHomes site. i will
certainly be checking in there. what i really think is
needed is an alternate -- or a constellation of
alternate -- approaches. for my tastes, preferably
a broad one; and i fear the circumspect naming of
the dreamhomes site, simply from the perspective
off wanting to cast as broad a net as is reasonable
and realistic. more on this below.]

- re PoMo architecture [btw, i am Heath, male, not
Heather. doesn't bug me, but thought i'd get it out
of the way.]: i live 3 blocks away from a Michael
Graves [the Central Denver Public Library, where i
work as a Children's Librarian] and, across the
street, the slowly mounting skeleton of what will be
the first Libeskind completed in the US; the
expansion of the Denver Art Museum. my affinity
for the Library is actually great [far greater now that
i have seen the new Seattle Public Alien Artefact ..
er, Library]; for the Libeskind, less so.. but slightly
moreso for the Libeskind than for the SPL, and for
most works of modernism... a pile of triangles vs a
pile of squares, i guess.. i think it is notable that
we can, do, and are able, to refer to these
structures as 'a Graves', 'a Libeskind', etc. to me,
this hints at the fact that they are meant to be
works of speculative art, largely, and that the
proper human context for works of art is as an
approachable object, about the size of a human,
not much larger than a room. even at that size,
their main [deliberate] effect is to seem very Other,
and that is sometimes appropriate, or at least
sometimes acceptable, as a work of art, but imho
fairly exasperating as an object and forum for of
human activity. now, i could actually go on and on
about how *humane* the Graves DPL building is in
contrast with what i've seen of much of his work, as
well as in contrast with the bulk of pomo, and the
reasons why i believe this happened. i probably will
someday. but not today.

having logged my own opinion, i would also note
the stance taken, largely, by New Urbanism, a
movement with which i am also quite sympathetic.
it is that one place where such works *may be*
appropriate, and one of the only places, is indeed
as large civic structures, positioned at the centers
or vertices of important boulevards and community
byways. these are spots where The Grand
Statement at least has some precedent [neoclassical
civic structures], and where the attention they draw
[gawking, really] may at least do some good for
their surrounding communities. in addition, they
are on some weird level preferable to the Vacant
Stare of Modernism; and we sense the inhumanity
of such boxes to a greater degree than their pomo
counterparts, which seem 'playful' to us. whether
they are human, or humane, is another issue; but at
any rate, NU feels that these sites are at least far
better than houses and bungalows, each one
screaming to make some pomo statement; one can
barely navigate the neighborhood above the din,
the argument goes.

so, there's all that.

---

intellectual property, copyright, patents, ownership
of concepts, etc:

maybe i worry too much about this, but i feel
strongly. today, i am not as worried about using
CES concepts as i was yesterday, simply after
reading further in NOO as to CA's hopes [mirrored
in APL] that these concepts could become starting
points for further exploration, elaboration, and
scientific discovery. at the very least, he seems
genuinely conflicted. :) but i do realize it is the
actual sequences he is talking about, and not the
concept of sequences. [in fact, i have, waiting in
the wings, a possible interdisciplinary silver bullet
tying a range of those concepts with an existing
approach in culture studies, but i will save that for
another time as well, since it's a sidetrack at this
point.]

as far as those go, i am dubious; quick research
claims that recipes for cooking are not patentable,
although one is counselled to reword recipes if one
is in doubt [sheesh]; meanwhile, another near
analogy, Origami seems rarely patentable, but
apparently attempts have been made. at this i can
only throw up my hands in exhasperation and
wonder.

as a creative person, i empathize with The Fear that
someone will 'steal my ideas'... but, i merely
empathize. it's no fun to be in fear. nor is this fear
necessary, imho. if one's work is designated Public
Domain [such a frightening, radical idea to most
that it seems to be a kind of political revolution in
itself], then yes, indeed, others might actually use
your ideas! and more, they might even reproduce
your ideas. they might even resell them. and if you
yourself, as the originator, are not already selling
your ideas as well, then shame on you. in such an
environment, attention [imho] settles out and is
paid where it belongs. those genuinely interested
will track down the originators of the ideas, to see
what they can about the context and environment
in which those ideas arose... and for reasons
mentioned by Brian; the physical representation of
an idea is but one small part of the subtle
knowledge which can bring those ideas to life. as
for those who do not care enough about the ideas
to track down their true source, well, they have
played their role in passing those ideas further on
than they would have gotten had that person never
encountered them in the first place. in that sense, i
think one of the very best and brightest strategies
for a conceptual framework which genuinely wishes
to spread, is to be Public Domain. or, for the sake
of argument, some other more reserved, but still
open-ended, scheme. for many, many alternatives,
as well as much more on this philosophy in general,
see:

http://www.creativecommons.org

which is scarier; the thought that someone might
rework and republish your idea, and thereby make
a few dollars on it, or the thought that you -- along
with any unpublished or even published-but-
circumspect ideas -- might die without ever having
reached others out there who desperately seek the
inspiration and crossovers your ideas might have
provided?

in publishing their own work, CES had a chance to
open that work up to these possibilities [not that
they or anyone else would have, but rather than
self-publishing is one of the *only* ways to PD or
CC your work in our intellectual-property-obsessed
time]. instead, i fear they seek the other direction,
simply out of lack of understanding, for that is the
only reason i can imagine for such a disconnect
between what the ideas point towards and what
extreme copyright points towards. copyright status
has *no bearing whatsoever* on what you yourself
can do with, how much money you can make off of,
how you can represent or reproduce, your ideas;
ONLY on what can be done with them afterwards.
for me, the advantages of reaching as wide [in time
as well as space] an audience as possible has
always outweighed my fears over making less
money on one of my ideas. then again, i took care
of seeking a career that would fulfill my desires to
enrich the world and also pay the bills partly so that
i could be freed from the fears which arise from
shackling my own success to control over my other
ideas.

I am using the ironworker to rapidly cut parts that are being used to fill the space inside scroll forms. To describe: I have some double scroll forms--that is two pieces of steel (forged to taper to a point and diminish in thickness as well to provide, looked at "calligraphically" different "weights" of line) welded together near the tips and bent into a scroll form together first, then one is bent away from its partner to form a space between the two pieces. This space varies from zero to maybe 2 1/2 inches. (Each set is different and unique.) To fill in these spaces I use the ironworker to rapidly cut pieces of steel to short (zero to 2 1/2") pieces that are then welded between the two scrolls to join them. This filling of the space is done with
1. various thicknesses
2. various spacings
3. various directions
4. various shapes Triangular/rectangular
5. triangles vary from wider bases to narrower bases

The result is that each of hundreds of scrolls is different. The ironworker makes it posssible to do this a lot cheaper, but it is still not within the range of anyone that does not have big bucks. Hopefully, my work is at least in sight of people on public streets, but in this case it is not. But I am very careful to avoid rubbber-stamp, rote detail if at all possible.

"I can see that my father, even and perhaps especially when in comeplete solitude, lives as a performer. It is a favorite request of his students to have him cut a notch completely with the axe rather than the chainsaw. From the rough cut to the razor sharp curve and smooth interior of the finished notch, he can still do it from start to finish without stopping, the axe biting cleanly along the pencil line of the scribe mark.
Watching him do it last winter, I asked how he could even see the line. He said he has never looked at the line. Students ask how he cuts the line, and he says he has no intention of hitting anywhere other than the line. The moment he launches the axe into it's curve of motion he releases all self-doubt and trusts himself to know exactly where the line is. He said at that moment he is not guiding the direction of the axe but instead following it."

I think it is fun to think about questions like the ones you pose about "nature" and gradually accruing abilities in the human form for manipulation of the environment, or Rob's about how centers and memory could be related.
I do not have much faith in the results of trying to answer such questions by ordinary human beings, because of the limits of our abilities to even observe what is going on. For example, Adi Da Samraj has pointed out that because our nervous system "takes time" to report to the brain, there is a built-in lag in our perception process such that we are never actually conscious of whatever the present Reality is. In a way, we are just not in a position to comment! To get an accurate idea of the Reality of the room you may be in at the moment, you would need to enjoy a perception of all possible "points of view" of that room. Our words are generated in a field of myriads of uninspected limits on our perception. The self-limit is a profound fault. Delusion, as Buddhists say. So, the unenlightened "point of view" is not equipped to understand the answers to the questions it may try to cook up. It is the frog in the well oblivious to the world outside, or the insect with no comprehension of winter, to refer to some of Ih Tiao Chang's quotations. So to really deal with such questions, I feel you have to deal with overcoming to self-limit. Of course, entrusting the "ego" to deal with itself is like asking Nixon to deal with Watergate, it's a joke. So, if one is serious, I believe one ends up seeking out a Guru. How one, already out of one's depth, could evaluate such a Guru accurately seems like a tall, paradoxical order for the self-limit to figure out. But, the egoic mind is not the final arbiter. There is exstacy (from I think Greek for standing (stasis) outside (ex) the self.) There is Love, there is Humor. Adi Da Samraj has told us that Feeling is "the hole in the Universe."
