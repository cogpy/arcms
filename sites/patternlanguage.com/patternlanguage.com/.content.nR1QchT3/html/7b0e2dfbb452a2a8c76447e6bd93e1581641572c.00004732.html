KEY ISSUES FOR A PEDESTRIAN STREET

Making decisions in the right order when you lay out (or improve) a pedestrian street controls the resulting liveliness. In some cases the "right" order is highly unexpected. Here are some of the aspects of the design sequence which must be done very early on:

  1. The position of the parking serving the pedestrian street
  2. The presence of an active center which draws people to it
  3. The location of the street with respect to the lines between the parking and the active center
If you start by doing these steps early on, and in the right order, there is a good chance the street will become lively. The active center and the parking dominate everything
