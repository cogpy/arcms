KEY ISSUES

Making decisions in the right order when you lay out (or improve) a pedestrian network in a neighborhood has major impact on the quality of community life in the neighborhood. In some cases the "right" order is unexpected. Here are some of the aspects of the action sequence which are most important:

  • Keep new pedestrian paths in spots that are beautiful NOW
  • Start with segments that form the "spine" of the neighborhood -- i.e. to places which everyone in the neighborhood naturally gets to
  • Use even little tiny bits -- if they are feasible NOW, they will do much to help the spirit, and encourage other bits to happen in future
  • Keep thinking about the connectivity of the neighborhood network as a whole
