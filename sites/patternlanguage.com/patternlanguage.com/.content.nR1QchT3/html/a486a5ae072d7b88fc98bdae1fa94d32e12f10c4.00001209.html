When I first met CA in 2004 and bought his books, I was irritated by the strength of his opinions and sceptical. Now three quarters of the way through the Nature of Order, I find it both profiound and right. As an architect at the end of my career, I recognise that the strengths of all the good things I have done are in line with his principles.
However, perhaps one has to distinguish between the principles and the way that CA himself does it. Each person must unfold his own destiny and method.

Dirk Visser 21:39:40 10/11/2005
I have followed the growth of Alexander's thought since the late sixties, and have never been disappointed in the sensitivity, rigorous clarity and simple directness of his vision.
The Nature of Order series is a triumph, a life's work in a class by itself.
The time will come when human thought grows to accept the inseperable identity of mind and logic, and interconnectedness of ideas and the material world.
Alexander's work in the Nature of Order series did cross that line in places, much to his credit, and it will be a valuable vantage point for decades to come as we explore this essential unity honestly for the first time.

Stephen Henderson 02:06:35 9/22/2005
Hey, this is Stephen Henderson, I'm a second year Architecture student at Texas A&M, I discovered the Nature of Order by chance on a stranger's blog-post. After that it was recommended by a favorite author/speaker's blog. So I was happy to find that my college library had the books.

Right now I'm about 80 pgs into book one and find it very interesting. As a Christian, I find it interesting how close he comes to the Christian worldveiw at times.

It is refreshing to see some-one else who is against the modern, and he is perceptive to trace bad architecture to a worldveiw based on false premises.

As someone once said, "Ideas have consequences".

Before you can think rightly about architecture, you must think rightly about the world around you, others, and yourself. Though he is reluctant to say it out right, I think our Worldveiws, and in turn Architecture, hang most primarally on the question of theology or atheology.

What is most Ultimate? God? Who is God? If there is no god, then what is most ultimate? The universe? What is it's nature? etc.
