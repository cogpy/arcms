CONTEXT The urban fringe: anywhere in the zone of land which is neither city nor country, that vast belt of half natural land forming the outer boundary of urbanized land, important for human use, often endangered by fire, a resource for animals and plant life that is most easily accessible to urban dwellers.
INPUT The opportunity to tame, or manage, or improve the scrubby and sometimes dangerous quality of this land.
OUTPUT A system of meadows, in which this land retains, or regains its beauty, yet draws on its natural qualities, through minimal intervention, thus creating hundreds of square miles of cultivated nature in close proximity to huge human populations.
