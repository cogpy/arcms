The Four Acre Process

A User-Initiated Process of Development

This is a process intended to create small-scale infill of groups of small houses, in small parcels of land which lie between development areas, and are close to existing communities or on their fringes.

The presumptions are as follows.

  • An average modest person (Mr Jones, not a developer) might typically be able to buy a piece of land anywhere from one acre to 10 acres. Averaging 4-5 acres. Typical price of such a parcel, at $8-10,000 per acre, would be about $40,000.(see appendix 1 for details)
  • Let assume this Mr Jones, buys such a parcel, or obtains an option on it.
  • He shows willingness to create a land trust, which will leave the land in a non-profit trust (will be done according to pre-drawn legal document costing him nothing, and free legal advice provided by the county).
  • Under the terms of this land trust, 50% of the land, the most beautiful part of it, is dedicated in perpetuity, for preservation as open land, used either as nature, or for small-holding agriculture, or food production.
  • First, the beautiful part, the 50% to be kept open, is chosen, and designated on the deed.
  • Very rough pedestrian streets, streets and boundaries are then recorded
The remainder -- the buildable part of this land -- is then to be configured by means of the following step-by-step process, as follows:
  • An announcement regarding ground rent. The Jones Trust, chaired by Mr Jones, announces the monthly ground rent, per sf, which will be charged for use of land in the buildable part.
  • Tenants -- would-be house-owners -- are invited to come forward, and join the Trust.
  • Instead of buying the land, they sign up for the right to use the land in perpetuity.
  • As would-be participants come forward, they are shown the loose layout of streets, common-land, and boundary of the buildable area, and select a site. They are free to choose position on a first-come first served basis, and may choose size and configuration according to rules laid down by the Trust (see appendix 2).
  • Broadly, they will set the boundary of their chosen parcels, with stakes, and these stakes will be recorded.
  • Parcels would tend to average about 1/4 acre: but the actual results would depend entirely on peoples individual judgement. and could run as low as 2000 sf, or as high as one acre.
  • The would-be owner will at the same time, define the position and rough size of house he intends to build
  • The process is simultaneously recorded on the web, and players, new houseowners, are able to see the plan evolving as new families join.
  • Once the families are assembled (not necessarily all, but up to some percentage), the housegroup goes for planning permission.
After permission, the individuals begin laying out their own houses, for themselves. And individual house layout process may be seen in appendix 3
  • This process is communal, but without forced community, People act individually, not in any forced kind of overly social atmosphere.
  • Yet at the same time, there is a relationship of each individual's action to the whole, thus creating a sense of mutual responsibility and community while leaving people and households their privacy of action.
