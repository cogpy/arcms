One of the most colorful and economically successful examples, of a commercial street fueled by the ideas of pattern language, was launched 20 years ago, and has almost legendary success today.

Entirely based on the ideas and concepts and tools of pattern language, the development was undertaken by developer Denny Abrams, formerly business manager of the Center for Environmental Structure, working for Christopher Alexander in the 1970's.

The most basic idea: Every decision taken benefits both the private individual, and the public good. The processes steer people towards the coupling of these two perspectives on every single decision.
